Privacy Policy

1. Introduction

VoteMe ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and what rights you have regarding your information when you use our electronic voting platform.

By registering an account or using VoteMe's services, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the platform.

2. Data We Collect

We collect only the minimum data necessary to provide the platform's services:

Account data: Your full name, email address, and hashed password when you register. This data is stored in our private PostgreSQL database and is never shared with third parties without your consent.

Voting data: When you participate in an election, we record your vote choice linked to your user account. This is required to enforce the one-vote-per-person rule and to prevent duplicate submissions. Vote choices are stored privately and are never displayed publicly alongside your identity.

Audit log data: All significant platform actions (account creation, login, vote submission, election lifecycle transitions) are recorded in an append-only audit log for transparency and accountability purposes.

Email address for notifications: If you are an organizer or voter in an active election, we may send transactional emails such as invitation notices, election reminders, and result announcements to the email address on your account.

3. Blockchain Transparency and Personal Data

VoteMe uses the Ethereum Sepolia testnet to record tamper-proof proofs of election parameters and results. Personal data is never stored on the blockchain.

What goes on-chain is limited to: a cryptographic hash of election configuration at the time of publication, and a cryptographic hash of final result data at the time of finalization. These hashes allow anyone to independently verify that neither the election rules nor the results were altered after publication, without exposing any personally identifiable information.

Because blockchain records are public and immutable, we take extra care to ensure that no names, emails, vote choices, or other personal identifiers are included in anything written to the blockchain.

4. Email Notifications

VoteMe sends transactional emails necessary for the operation of the platform, including:

Account confirmation emails when you register; password reset links when requested; election invitation emails when an organizer invites you to participate; reminder emails before an election closes; and result announcement emails when an election you participated in is finalized.

We do not send marketing or promotional emails without your explicit consent. You can contact us at votemeinfonsupport@gmail.com to manage your notification preferences or to request removal from our mailing list.

5. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal data held by VoteMe:

Right to access: You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you at any time.

Right to correction: You may update your name and email address from your profile settings. If you need to correct other data, contact us directly.

Right to deletion: You may request deletion of your account and associated personal data. Please note that audit log entries and blockchain records (which contain no personal data) cannot be deleted due to their immutable nature.

Right to data portability: You may request an export of your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at votemeinfonsupport@gmail.com.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These include HTTPS encryption for all data in transit, hashed password storage, JWT-based authentication tokens, and access controls enforced at both the application and database levels.

No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a data breach that affects your rights or freedoms, we will notify you and relevant authorities as required by applicable law.
